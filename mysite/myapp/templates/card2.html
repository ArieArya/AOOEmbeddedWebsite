YogaTech.

How it works

YogaTech is built using the latest Raspberry-Pi Zero with a 1GHz single-core CPU. By designing the platform and software with multi-threading and concurrency in mind, a far improved efficiency is achieved that enables real-time signal processing and live audio feedback. In addition, the Adafruit LIS3DH accelerometer is used to achieve accurate motion tracking of your yoga poses - which is then compared to optimal expert poses to assess your performance and give immediate personalized feedback.

Tools and Libraries

The main high-level language used in the platform is Python, which runs on the Raspberry-Pi Zero along with its various libraries; the most crucial of which includes NumPy for matrix processing, JSON to encapsulate data to transfer via MQTT, Tkinter for the main UI, and Matplotlib to display your performance throughout the session. The three main building blocks of the device include: Motion Tracking and Analysis, MQTT messaging protocol, and the Real-time audio feedback.

Motion Tracking and Analysis

Through the triple-axis Adafruit LIS3DH accelerometer, our device is able to achieve accurate motion tracking of your yoga poses. After careful data pre-processing, we turn the raw accelerometer data from cartesian to polar coordinates to compare your performance to ideal positions of the specific pose you selected. This will then be sent back to your mobile device via the MQTT protocol to give you live feedback of your performance.

MQTT Lightweight Messaging Protocol

The MQTT lightweight messaging protocol over SSL is a versatile protocol used in various IoT applications, allowing your mobile phone to communicate with our device at a high bandwidth. Moreover, through the use of client certificates and the MQTT publish and subscribe mechanism, the communication between your mobile phone and our device will be both efficient and secure.

Real-time Audio Feedback

The data accumulated through the LIS3DH accelerometer will be pre-processed and sent periodically to your mobile device via the MQTT protocol. Using this data, we are able to track your overall performance and give on-the-spot feedback on how you can improve your pose. In addition, after your session is completed, a final overall feedback and score will be given to allow you to improve in future yoga sessions.
